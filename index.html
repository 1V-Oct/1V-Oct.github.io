1V/OCT aka One Volt Per Octave

/wʌn voʊlt pər ˈɒk.tɪv/

The name refers to both our brand and a fundamental term in modular synthesis that describes how pitch is controlled.

1V/octave (1 Volt per Octave)

The 1V/oct standard is the most widely adopted control voltage scaling convention in modern modular synthesizers. It defines a linear relationship between control voltage and musical pitch, such that:

  • An increase of exactly 1 volt corresponds to a precise rise of one octave in pitch.
  • Consequently, a change of approximately 83.33 mV (1/12 V) produces a shift of one semitone.

This exponential (logarithmic in frequency) voltage-to-pitch scaling ensures accurate and predictable tracking across multiple octaves when connecting controllers, sequencers, keyboard interfaces, or quantizers to voltage-controlled oscillators (VCOs), filters, or other 1V/oct-compatible modules.

The 1V/oct standard provides excellent compatibility between equipment from different manufacturers and is the de facto industry norm in contemporary Eurorack and most other modular formats.

(Contrast with the older Hz/Volt system, which uses linear frequency scaling and is found primarily in certain vintage or specialized synthesizers.)
